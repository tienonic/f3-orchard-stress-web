# Final Report Guide

Include the interactive field app near the top of the final report, ideally after the executive summary and before the detailed methods. The app is the fastest way for a reviewer to understand how the model output becomes a field workflow.

Live app: https://f3-orchard-stress-web.vercel.app

Public source: https://github.com/tienonic/f3-orchard-stress-web

## Suggested Report Section

Use a section titled "Interactive Field Quick Start".

The section should say that the app turns mapped orchard stress zones into inspection targets. A user can choose a site, inspect the colored zones, click an exact point of interest, read the selected coordinates, and open Google Maps to route to that point.

## Quick Start Copy

1. Open the live app.
2. Start with Partner Site 1, then use the selector for the larger public-boundary examples.
3. Hover on desktop, or tap and drag on mobile, to read coordinates.
4. Select a red or orange zone to see whether it is a scouting priority.
5. Click any exact point of interest to refine the navigation coordinate.
6. Use "Yes, open route" to open Google Maps to the selected coordinate.

## Why It Matters

- It turns a static stress map into a field-ready navigation tool.
- It makes the next inspection step obvious for growers and field scouts.
- It supports faster comparison between weak canopy and nearby reference canopy.
- It gives a practical place to collect field feedback and improve thresholds later.

## Interpretation Notes

- Red "Scout first" zones are the first areas to inspect.
- Orange "Monitor" zones are secondary checks if time allows.
- Gray "Stable context" zones are useful comparison areas, not problem calls.
- Green "Strong reference" zones are healthy-looking comparison areas.
- The app prioritizes field follow-up. It does not diagnose disease, pests, irrigation failure, nutrient issues, soil conditions, yield loss, or legal field access.

## Final Check

Before submission, confirm the live app opens, the README links are visible near the top, the report includes the app link, and the Google Maps route uses the selected latitude and longitude.
